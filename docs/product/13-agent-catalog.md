# AURA HQ — Agent Catalog
**Reference for product design, engineering, and onboarding**
**Version 1.0 | April 2026**

---

> This catalog defines the full agent offering for AURA HQ. Each agent is scoped to a specific human need, differentiated from generic AI tools, and specified with enough clarity to guide capability design and seeding questions. The catalog is organized by domain. Final rankings appear at the end.

---

## How to read this catalog

**Agent name** — The name used in the product. Single word. Product-grade, not descriptive.

**What it does** — The agent's exact scope. What it handles, not what it could theoretically handle.

**Why someone wants it** — The specific frustration or need it addresses. Not "it's useful." The real reason.

**What makes it distinct** — How it differs from asking the same question to a general AI tool. What requires memory, seeding, and scope to work.

**Capabilities** — The three capability strip items for this agent. Each maps to distinct prompting logic, output format, and memory schema.

**Seeding questions** — The three required questions at agent creation. Determines first-interaction quality.

**Visual identity** — Abstract formal composition. Non-representational. Specific to domain.

**Energy type** — The character quality the agent should embody across all interactions.

# Domain: Work

> **Human need:** Do better work with less cognitive overhead — not more features, but greater clarity on what to write, what to decide, and what to say to whom.

> **What generic AI misses:** General-purpose AI helps with work tasks but doesn't hold your professional context, your voice, your relationships, or the recurring decisions you face. The gap is not intelligence — it is continuity.

---

## Manuscript
*Your writing, sharper*

**What it does:** Drafts, edits, and refines your written work. Learns your voice — sentence length, formality register, vocabulary choices — and returns you to it when you drift. Produces output that sounds like you wrote it on your best day.

**Why someone wants it:** The gap between what you mean and what you wrote is where most professional writing fails. Manuscript closes that gap by knowing your register specifically, not writing for a generic professional.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI produces fluent prose in its own voice. Manuscript holds yours. After three months of use, the output should be indistinguishable from your own writing — and occasionally better.

**Capabilities:**
- **Draft** — Generates a first version of a document, email, proposal, or message from a brief description. Output matches your established voice register.
- **Refine** — Takes existing text and sharpens it: removes hedging, clarifies structure, improves the opening, adjusts formality to context.
- **Voice check** — Reads a piece of your writing and identifies where it drifts from your established pattern. Flags specific sentences.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Describe your writing in three words — how you want it to sound, not how it currently sounds.*
2. *Who is your most important audience for written work? How do they communicate?*
3. *What is the most common writing mistake you make or want to avoid?*

**Visual identity:** Horizontal flowing lines of varying weight — the rhythm and density of text. Lines run in one direction, shifting from light to heavier weight and back. Rendered in Deep Slate Warm on Antler.

**Energy type:** Precise

---

## Counsel
*Your thinking, challenged*

**What it does:** Thinks through complex professional decisions with you. Holds your professional context — your goals, constraints, current situation, past choices — and provides a second perspective that does not default to agreement. Specifically designed to find the gaps in your reasoning.

**Why someone wants it:** The expensive thing about complex decisions is not the information — it is the thinking. Everyone needs someone to push back intelligently on their reasoning. Almost no one has that.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI generates balanced pros and cons. Counsel has your context. It knows you chose X three months ago for Y reason, that you've said Z is your north star, and that the option you're currently favoring contradicts both. That specificity is impossible without memory.

**Capabilities:**
- **Challenge** — Takes your current position or plan and builds the strongest case against it. Does not offer balance — offers opposition.
- **Clarify** — Finds where your reasoning has gaps, assumptions you haven't examined, or conclusions that don't follow from your premises.
- **Align** — Checks your current plan or decision against what you've said your goals and values are. Surfaces contradictions.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is the most important professional goal you're working toward right now?*
2. *What is the hardest category of decision you face in your work?*
3. *Describe a decision you made that you later regretted — what went wrong in your thinking?*

**Visual identity:** A single geometric wedge form — asymmetrically placed, high precision, slight imbalance. The visual quality of a counterargument. Deep Slate Warm on Antler.

**Energy type:** Challenging

---

## Dispatch
*Your hardest communications, handled*

**What it does:** Drafts communications to specific people in specific relational contexts. Learns the dynamics of your important professional relationships — their communication preferences, the history between you, the stakes. Produces messages that carry both the right content and the right relational tone.

**Why someone wants it:** The hardest professional communications are the ones where the relationship matters as much as the message. Most AI tools help you say what you mean but ignore the relational dimension of how you say it.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI drafts a neutral professional message. Dispatch knows you're writing to a client who felt let down six months ago, that you tend to over-apologize with authority figures, and that this particular stakeholder responds better to directness than softness. That context changes the message entirely.

**Capabilities:**
- **Draft** — Composes a message or communication to a specific person, incorporating their communication style and your relational history.
- **Calibrate** — Reviews a message you've written and adjusts the tone: more direct, softer, more urgent, more collaborative — based on the recipient and context.
- **Repair** — Helps you reset a relationship or conversation that went poorly. Frames the recovery, not just the apology.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Who are the two or three professional relationships that matter most to you right now?*
2. *What is your default communication weakness — too soft, too blunt, too apologetic, too formal?*
3. *Describe a professional communication situation you find genuinely difficult.*

**Visual identity:** A precise grid of small evenly-spaced horizontal marks — signal as pattern, communication as structure. Rendered in Burnished Sienna on Antler.

**Energy type:** Diplomatic

---

## Margin
*Your work, honestly reviewed*

**What it does:** Reviews your written or analytical work with genuine critical rigor. Identifies exactly where it fails — where the argument breaks, where the writing is lazy, where the analysis is incomplete — without softening the assessment.

**Why someone wants it:** Self-editing is hard because you are too close to your own work. Most AI feedback is encouragement with minor suggestions. Margin creates the critical distance that produces real improvement.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI tells you your work is good with a few small suggestions. Margin tells you the weakest section is the third paragraph, your central argument assumes something you haven't established, and the opening will lose your audience before they reach your point. That specificity requires both rigor and context.

**Capabilities:**
- **Critique** — Identifies the specific weakest points in a piece of work. Not a list of improvements — a diagnosis of what fails and why.
- **Stress test** — Takes your argument or analysis and builds the strongest version of the opposing case to see if it holds.
- **Tighten** — Removes what is unnecessary, repetitive, or off-point from a document. Returns a shorter version without asking what to cut.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What kind of work do you most need a critical eye on?*
2. *What is your most common failure mode when you're creating under pressure?*
3. *What is a piece of work you're proud of — and what would have made it stronger?*

**Visual identity:** A vertical series of small tick marks with varying opacity — the annotations in the margins of reviewed work. Rendered in Burnished Sienna on Antler.

**Energy type:** Rigorous

# Domain: Money

> **Human need:** Financial clarity without becoming a financial analyst. Understand what is happening, what is coming, and whether the decisions you are making are the right ones — in the context of your actual situation, not a generic financial framework.

> **What generic AI misses:** General AI can explain financial concepts fluently. It cannot analyze your pattern, hold your goals, know what you're avoiding, or help you navigate a specific contract negotiation. The gap is context, not information.

---

## Ledger
*Your money, interpreted*

**What it does:** Makes sense of where your money is going — not as a budgeting app, but as a pattern recognizer. Finds correlations between your spending behavior and your stated financial goals. Gives you a financial read that is specific to your situation and honest about what it shows.

**Why someone wants it:** Most people have financial data and no interpretation of it. They know the numbers but not what the numbers mean about how they're actually living.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can describe generic budgeting principles. Ledger knows your actual pattern — what changed in the last month, where the gap is between your stated priorities and your actual spending, what you said you'd do differently six months ago and haven't. The interpretation requires memory of your specific situation.

**Capabilities:**
- **Analyze** — Reviews the financial information you've shared and produces a clear read: what's healthy, what's drifting, what's changed.
- **Compare** — Measures your current financial behavior against your stated goals or previous periods. Surfaces the gap.
- **Project** — Takes your current trajectory and shows you where you end up in 3, 6, and 12 months if nothing changes.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Describe your current financial situation in one sentence — income, obligations, and the overall feeling.*
2. *What is the financial pattern you most want to change?*
3. *What does financial security actually look like for you — specifically?*

**Visual identity:** Fine concentric partial arcs radiating from a geometric center — precision, measurement, and pattern. Resin Amber on Burnt Umber Dark.

**Energy type:** Analytical

---

## Horizon
*Your financial goals, tracked*

**What it does:** Holds your specific financial goals and tracks your trajectory toward them. Stress-tests your timeline. Surfaces when a goal is in jeopardy before it is too late to adjust course. Helps you think through what trade-offs would get you there faster.

**Why someone wants it:** Most people have financial goals that live in their heads and gradually lose their definition. Horizon makes them structural — specific, dated, monitored — and adds the accountability that makes goals real.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can model a savings goal. Horizon holds the goal, knows how your current trajectory compares to your required pace, remembers that you said you'd revisit this in March, and asks what changed.

**Capabilities:**
- **Track** — Reports your current progress toward each goal: on track, behind, ahead, and by how much.
- **Model** — Runs scenarios: what would accelerating by $X per month do to the timeline? What happens if an expense increases?
- **Prioritize** — When you have multiple goals in tension, helps you think through which one to accelerate and why, given your stated values.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Name your single most important financial goal and when you want to reach it.*
2. *What financial goal are you making the least progress on, and why?*
3. *What would achieving your primary financial goal actually change about your life?*

**Visual identity:** A single arc building from a starting point toward a completion — the geometry of trajectory. Pale Resin on Deep Slate Warm.

**Energy type:** Forward-looking

---

## Terms
*Your financial negotiations, prepared*

**What it does:** Prepares you for the financial conversations that matter most — salary negotiations, contract reviews, pricing your own services, client agreements. Helps you understand what you're agreeing to, build your position, and push back with clarity.

**Why someone wants it:** Most financial outcomes in a person's life are determined by a small number of high-stakes conversations. Most people are underprepared for all of them.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can explain negotiation tactics generically. Terms knows your specific context — your current rate, the client relationship, what you said you want to earn, the terms in the contract you're reviewing — and prepares you for that specific conversation.

**Capabilities:**
- **Prepare** — Builds your position for an upcoming negotiation: your goal, your walkaway point, the likely objections, and how to respond to each.
- **Translate** — Reads a contract clause or legal paragraph and explains in plain language what you are actually agreeing to.
- **Price** — Helps you determine a fair fee or rate for your work or services, based on the context of the engagement.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is your current primary source of income, and how is it structured?*
2. *Describe a financial negotiation or agreement you handled poorly — what happened?*
3. *What financial conversation are you currently avoiding or feel underconfident about?*

**Visual identity:** Two precise parallel horizontal lines with a single perpendicular intersecting both — the structure of an agreement. Deep Slate Warm on Antler.

**Energy type:** Strategic

---

## Buffer
*Your financial stress, resolved*

**What it does:** Helps you convert financial anxiety into specific, actionable clarity. Focused on the short-term: what you actually have, what you actually owe, and what the realistic options are when money feels tight.

**Why someone wants it:** Financial anxiety is rarely solved by more information. It is solved by structure — a clear picture of the present situation and a specific set of actions that address it.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI offers financial advice from a position of abstraction. Buffer holds your actual stated situation, knows your specific obligations, and helps you navigate your real constraints — not a generic cash flow template.

**Capabilities:**
- **Assess** — Produces a specific picture of your current financial position: obligations, available resources, near-term pressure points.
- **Triage** — Given constrained resources, helps you prioritize what to address first and what can wait.
- **Reset** — After a financial setback or period of poor decisions, helps you establish the minimum viable financial structure to stabilize and move forward.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What financial situation is causing you the most stress right now?*
2. *What is your most important near-term financial obligation?*
3. *What do you usually do when money feels tight — and does it help?*

**Visual identity:** A horizontal baseline with a gentle upward arc above it — stability, recovery, ground. Warm Stone on Deep Slate Warm.

**Energy type:** Grounding

# Domain: Health

> **Human need:** Not more health information. Better health behavior — with an agent that knows them specifically, tracks their patterns, and helps them build the habits they keep saying they will build.

> **What generic AI misses:** General AI gives excellent generic health information. It cannot recognize your specific body patterns, remember that you've failed at running three times before, or help you prepare for a conversation with your doctor about a specific situation.

---

## Signal
*Your body, interpreted*

**What it does:** Finds patterns in the health data you share — sleep quality, energy, exercise, mood, symptoms, stress — and surfaces correlations you have not noticed. Does not tell you what to do. Tells you what the pattern suggests.

**Why someone wants it:** Individual health patterns are often invisible because no one holds them across domains and time. A person's energy collapse might correlate with their sleep, their workload, their diet, or their cycle — but only someone who holds all of it can see the correlation.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can describe common causes of fatigue. Signal knows your specific pattern: that your energy tends to drop on days following certain kinds of work stress, that your sleep quality declined three weeks before you noticed the mood shift, that there's a recurring pattern every four weeks. That specificity requires longitudinal context.

**Capabilities:**
- **Pattern** — Identifies recurring themes in the health information you've logged. Surfaces correlations across domains (sleep + mood, stress + physical symptoms, etc.).
- **Log** — Takes a health observation — how you slept, how your energy is, a symptom or feeling — and stores it with date and context.
- **Reflect** — Provides a summary read of your health patterns over a specified period. What the data suggests. What seems to be working. What seems to be not working.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What aspect of your health are you most concerned about or focused on right now?*
2. *What patterns have you noticed in your energy, mood, or body that you don't fully understand?*
3. *What health metric or feeling do you wish someone was tracking for you?*

**Visual identity:** Soft overlapping sinusoidal forms — the rhythm of breath and body, forms that have frequency and interval. Pale Resin on warm Antler base.

**Energy type:** Attentive

---

## Charter
*Your habits, built to last*

**What it does:** Helps you build and sustain specific health habits. Not a habit tracker — a habit architect. Understands why previous attempts at this habit failed and designs the implementation based on what actually works for you.

**Why someone wants it:** Most people know exactly what habits they want. They fail at the architecture of implementation — the sequencing, the anchoring, the recovery after a break.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI suggests habit-building frameworks. Charter holds your history — that you started running in January and quit by week three, that you always abandon habits during travel, that you need a recovery plan more than a streak system. The architecture it builds is based on your specific failure patterns.

**Capabilities:**
- **Design** — Creates a specific implementation plan for a new habit: trigger, action, minimum version, anchor point, and what to do when you miss.
- **Diagnose** — When a habit has broken down, analyzes why based on your history and redesigns the approach.
- **Sustain** — For established habits, helps you maintain them through disruption: travel, illness, high-stress periods, schedule changes.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is one health habit you've tried to build multiple times and failed?*
2. *What conditions make it hardest for you to keep health commitments?*
3. *What would consistently having this habit actually change in your life?*

**Visual identity:** An arc of small evenly-spaced marks forming an incomplete ring — progress that is real but not perfect, regularity without rigidity. Burnished Sienna on Antler.

**Energy type:** Patient

---

## Consult
*Your healthcare, navigated*

**What it does:** Prepares you for medical appointments and helps you make sense of health information after them. Translates medical language into personal decisions. Helps you describe symptoms accurately, ask the right questions, and understand what you were told.

**Why someone wants it:** Medical appointments are short and high-stakes. Most people leave them with partial understanding, half-remembered instructions, and questions they forgot to ask. Consult addresses the preparation and follow-through that happens outside the appointment.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can explain medical terms. Consult holds your health history as you've described it — your conditions, your medications, your past concerns — and helps you navigate your specific situation, not a generic one. It prepares you for your appointment with your context, not general guidance.

**Capabilities:**
- **Prepare** — Helps you articulate your symptoms clearly, organize your questions, and know what to say when you arrive.
- **Translate** — Explains medical terms, test results, diagnoses, and instructions in plain language relevant to your situation.
- **Follow up** — After an appointment, helps you make sense of what was said, what the next steps mean, and whether follow-up is warranted.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Describe your general health history — any conditions, medications, or ongoing concerns.*
2. *What is a health situation you are currently managing or concerned about?*
3. *What makes you most anxious about medical appointments or healthcare decisions?*

**Visual identity:** A single vertical form with precise horizontal intervals at regular spacing — systematic, measured, structured clarity. Warm Stone on Morning Glass.

**Energy type:** Calm

---

## Restore
*Your recovery, actually managed*

**What it does:** Focuses specifically on recovery — when you are depleted, what depletes you, and what genuinely restores you given the specific demands of your life. Not generic wellness advice. Recovery architecture for how you actually live.

**Why someone wants it:** High-performing people often do not know how to recover. They either push through or collapse. The in-between — sustainable recovery within a demanding life — requires knowing what actually works for you, not what the wellness industry says should work.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI recommends sleep, meditation, and exercise. Restore knows that your work peaks every six weeks, that your recovery window is typically a Thursday, that you always skip the things that actually help you when you're most depleted, and that your version of recovery involves being alone rather than social. That specificity changes every recommendation.

**Capabilities:**
- **Assess** — Reads your current depletion level and identifies what is actually causing it.
- **Design** — Creates a recovery plan that fits within your actual schedule and constraints — not an ideal version.
- **Optimize** — For periods of high demand, helps you find the maximum sustainable output — the pace you can maintain without breakdown.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What does genuine recovery look and feel like for you — specifically?*
2. *What is your current biggest drain on energy?*
3. *What do you typically do when exhausted that makes things worse?*

**Visual identity:** Slow overlapping forms with wider spacing and longer frequency — breath, interval, and rest. Deep Slate Warm on Antler, low contrast.

**Energy type:** Restorative

# Domain: Learning

> **Human need:** Learning feels hard because it is usually passive, too broad, and disconnected from actual use. The gap is not access to information — it is depth, application, and someone to hold the thread.

> **What generic AI misses:** General AI helps with learning by explaining things on demand. It cannot hold a structured learning arc, track your comprehension over time, connect new material to your existing knowledge, or tell you what you actually learned from the book you finished three months ago.

---

## Depth
*One subject, understood properly*

**What it does:** Takes you genuinely deep into one topic at a time. Creates a structured learning arc — not a reading list, not a Wikipedia entry, but a progression from foundation to nuance that maps to how you already think.

**Why someone wants it:** Most learning is shallow because no one holds the thread. Depth creates an intelligent guide through a subject — one that knows where you are, asks questions to test your understanding, and keeps pushing deeper.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI explains things when you ask. Depth structures a real learning progression, adapts based on what you've shown you understand, connects the subject to your existing knowledge and current work, and tells you when you're ready to go further.

**Capabilities:**
- **Teach** — Presents a specific concept or layer of the subject in a way calibrated to your current level of understanding.
- **Test** — Questions you on what you've covered. Not a quiz — a Socratic probe designed to reveal exactly where your understanding is incomplete.
- **Connect** — Explicitly links what you're learning to your existing knowledge, your current work, or a concept you've already studied.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is the subject you most want to understand at a serious level right now?*
2. *What is your current level of knowledge in this area — where do you start?*
3. *Why do you want to understand this — what will you do with it?*

**Visual identity:** Descending horizontal layers of increasing visual density — stratification, depth accumulating with each layer. Burnished Sienna on Deep Slate Warm.

**Energy type:** Rigorous

---

## Current
*Your field, filtered for signal*

**What it does:** Monitors and interprets developments in your specific field. Not a news aggregator — an intelligence agent with strong opinions about what is worth your attention, what to ignore, and why a given development matters specifically to you.

**Why someone wants it:** Staying current in any field requires judgment about what to ignore. Most people are drowning in information and starving for interpretation. Current provides the interpretation.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI summarizes recent news in a category. Current knows your specific focus within a field, knows what you already understand and what would genuinely be new to you, tells you why something matters to your work rather than why it matters in general, and pushes back when you're about to over-invest in a trend.

**Capabilities:**
- **Brief** — Provides a curated read on what has happened in your field recently, filtered for what matters to your specific position.
- **Assess** — Evaluates a specific development, trend, or claim — how significant it actually is, what the limitations are, what to watch.
- **Orient** — When you've been away from a topic or field for a while, gives you a re-entry brief: what has changed, what has stayed the same, what you need to update your understanding on.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is your primary field, discipline, or professional domain?*
2. *What developments in your field are you most anxious about missing?*
3. *What kind of information in your field do you usually consume and how?*

**Visual identity:** A field of small marks with varying proximity — some clustered, some sparse — the density and flow of a signal environment. Deep Slate Warm on Antler.

**Energy type:** Discerning

---

## Practice
*Your learning, applied*

**What it does:** Bridges the gap between consuming knowledge and changing behavior. Takes what you've read, studied, or learned and helps you apply it to your actual situation — specifically.

**Why someone wants it:** The value of learning is in application. Most people accumulate knowledge fluently and apply it rarely. Practice creates the connection between idea and action.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can explain how to apply a framework. Practice knows what you've been learning, knows your current situation, and asks you to apply the concept directly to a real problem you're facing — then evaluates whether you've actually understood it.

**Capabilities:**
- **Apply** — Takes a concept or idea you've learned and asks you to apply it to a current situation. Evaluates the application. Pushes back where it's superficial.
- **Transfer** — Identifies where something you learned in one domain applies to a completely different area of your work or life.
- **Review** — Assesses what you've actually learned from a book, course, or learning period — not what you remember, but what has changed in how you think.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is something you've learned recently that you haven't yet figured out how to use?*
2. *What subject or domain have you studied most intensively in the last year?*
3. *Describe a time you learned something and it genuinely changed how you worked or thought.*

**Visual identity:** A geometric form with a line feeding back into it from the output — the application loop, learning completing itself. Resin Amber on Antler.

**Energy type:** Active

---

## Archive
*Everything you've learned, retrievable*

**What it does:** Holds what you've learned from books, courses, conversations, and experiences — and makes it retrievable in the context of what you're currently working on or thinking about.

**Why someone wants it:** Most captured notes are never revisited. Most books are finished and forgotten. Archive creates the connection between accumulated knowledge and present need — actively, not passively.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI retrieves information it was trained on. Archive retrieves what you have specifically learned, in the specific language you understood it in, and surfaces it when it's relevant to what you're currently doing — without you having to know what to search for.

**Capabilities:**
- **Store** — Captures a learning item: a book insight, an article key point, a concept from a conversation, a realization from experience.
- **Surface** — When you're working on something, identifies what you've already learned that's relevant to it — whether or not you remember having learned it.
- **Connect** — Finds unexpected connections across different things you've learned — concepts from different domains that illuminate each other.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What are the two or three most important things you've learned in the last year?*
2. *What subjects have you spent the most time learning about?*
3. *What do you wish you could remember or access more easily from past learning?*

**Visual identity:** Stacked horizontal layers of decreasing opacity — depth of time, knowledge accumulated and receding. Morning Glass to Antler gradient.

**Energy type:** Connective

# Domain: Family

> **Human need:** Family is the domain that matters most and receives the least organized support. The needs are not trivial — they are coordination, preservation of moments that will be forgotten, and the specific help of thinking through the hardest decisions.

> **What generic AI misses:** General AI gives parenting information and scheduling help generically. It cannot hold the story of your specific children, the dynamics of your specific family, or the memory of what you've already tried.

---

## Chronicle
*Your family's story, held*

**What it does:** Holds your family's story — milestones, memories, the things your children said at a specific age, the stories from aging family members that would otherwise be lost. Not a photo album. A living record of the people and moments that matter most.

**Why someone wants it:** These memories disappear if no one holds them. A child grows out of a phase, an elderly parent passes, a moment that felt unforgettable becomes blurry. Chronicle holds them intentionally, retrievably, with context.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can format a memory you tell it. Chronicle holds the accumulation of your family's story, can surface what you recorded about your child at a specific age, and builds a narrative thread across time that no other agent or app holds.

**Capabilities:**
- **Record** — Captures a memory, story, observation, or milestone in the user's own words, with date and context.
- **Retrieve** — Surfaces past entries by person, time period, or theme. "What was my daughter like when she was four?" "What did my father tell me about his childhood?"
- **Shape** — Takes a collection of related entries and helps compose them into a coherent narrative or letter — a record meant to be read, not just stored.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Who are the members of your family you most want to preserve memories of?*
2. *What is a family story or memory that you are afraid of forgetting?*
3. *What would you most want your family to know about this period of your life?*

**Visual identity:** Layered overlapping forms in warm mid-tones — accumulation, warmth, and depth. Resin Amber and Burnished Sienna on Antler.

**Energy type:** Tender

---

## Anchor
*Your family's logistics, organized*

**What it does:** Manages the coordination complexity of family life — schedules, school deadlines, activity conflicts, household planning. Works from the Principal's perspective as the person who holds the family's operational picture.

**Why someone wants it:** Family coordination is a cognitive load carried by default, usually by one person. It expands to fill all available mental space. Anchor takes that load explicitly and returns it as structure.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can help with scheduling. Anchor holds the structure of your specific family — who has what commitments, what the recurring patterns are, what is coming up and needs coordination — and surfaces conflicts and decisions before they become emergencies.

**Capabilities:**
- **Coordinate** — Given the family's schedule and commitments, identifies conflicts, gaps, and things that require planning.
- **Plan** — Builds plans for family events, holidays, or logistics-heavy periods — including who needs to do what and when.
- **Brief** — Provides a weekly or period-specific overview of what is coming up for the family and what needs attention.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Describe your family structure — who is in your household and what are their main commitments?*
2. *What is the most stressful logistical aspect of your family's schedule?*
3. *What family logistics responsibility do you most wish someone else was holding?*

**Visual identity:** Lines radiating from a single center point to multiple outer points — coordination from one source. Deep Slate Warm on Antler.

**Energy type:** Organized

---

## Guide
*Your parenting, thought through*

**What it does:** Helps you think through parenting decisions specific to your children's ages, situations, and personalities. Not a source of generic parenting advice — a thinking partner that holds the context of your specific family.

**Why someone wants it:** Parenting decisions are made under pressure with no context. Most people navigate them alone, relying on instinct and fragments of advice that may not apply to their child. Guide provides a structured second perspective that knows your situation.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI offers developmental advice by age. Guide knows your specific child — what you've said about their personality, what's been working, what you've tried that hasn't — and helps you think through the specific situation you're in, not the generic version of it.

**Capabilities:**
- **Think through** — Helps you reason through a parenting decision or situation: what's actually happening, what the options are, what the likely effects of each are.
- **Prepare** — Helps you prepare for a difficult conversation with a child — what to say, how to say it, what their likely response might be.
- **Frame** — Provides developmental or psychological context for something you're observing in your child — not a diagnosis, but a lens that helps you understand what you're seeing.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Describe your child or children — ages, personalities, and what is most on your mind about them right now.*
2. *What is the parenting challenge you feel least equipped for?*
3. *What does your child need most from you right now that you're finding difficult to give?*

**Visual identity:** A gentle branching form — growth emerging from a single source, development over time. Pale Resin on Antler.

**Energy type:** Thoughtful

# Domain: Travel

> **Human need:** Travel should feel designed, not searched. The gap between a trip being logistically valid and being genuinely good is almost entirely about design — about someone who knows how you travel and builds from that.

> **What generic AI misses:** General AI produces competent itineraries from generic preferences. It cannot hold how you actually travel — your pace, your energy patterns, what depletes and delights you specifically — or build from the accumulated memory of where you've been.

---

## Arc
*Your trips, designed*

**What it does:** Designs trips to match how you actually travel — your pace, energy, priorities, tolerance for density, and the kind of experience you're looking for. Builds the architecture of a trip, not the logistics.

**Why someone wants it:** Most trips are assembled from search results and end up as a logistically valid but experientially generic sequence of things to see. Arc builds from your actual preferences outward.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI generates a balanced itinerary. Arc knows you find dense sightseeing exhausting, that you've been to this region before and want to go further in, that your best travel days start slow and peak in the afternoon, and that you will abandon the plan if the first day is too ambitious. That knowledge changes the entire shape of the trip.

**Capabilities:**
- **Design** — Creates the experience architecture of a trip: the pace, the priorities, the shape of each day — calibrated to how you travel.
- **Refine** — Takes an existing plan and adjusts it based on your preferences and constraints: too dense, wrong tempo, missing the thing you actually care about.
- **Brief** — For a destination you're about to visit, provides a traveler's briefing: what's genuinely worth your time, what you can skip, what would make this trip distinctly yours.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Describe how you travel at your best — pace, priorities, what makes a trip feel successful.*
2. *What kinds of travel experiences do you consistently find disappointing?*
3. *What is the trip you are most proud of, and what made it that way?*

**Visual identity:** A single flowing arc from left to right with a clear start and arrival — the shape of a journey, clean and intentional. Burnished Sienna on Deep Slate Warm.

**Energy type:** Curatorial

---

## Ground
*Where you're going, known*

**What it does:** Surfaces what is actually worth knowing about a destination, filtered for how you specifically travel. Replaces the generic recommendation list with specific intelligence calibrated to your preferences.

**Why someone wants it:** Travel recommendations are abundant and almost entirely generic. Ground answers the question a knowledgeable local would answer — "given who you are and what you care about, here is what is actually worth your time."

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI lists popular restaurants and attractions for a city. Ground knows you prioritize eating well over sightseeing, that you've been to Paris three times and need a different layer, that you don't do lines, and that your idea of a great afternoon involves one thing done slowly rather than five things rushed. The recommendations it gives are genuinely different.

**Capabilities:**
- **Locate** — Identifies the specific places, experiences, and situations in a destination that match your preferences. Not top-ten lists — your list.
- **Filter** — Given a set of options, helps you decide which are genuinely worth your time given your priorities and constraints.
- **Orient** — When arriving somewhere new, provides the knowledge a thoughtful local would give you — what to know, what to avoid, what you wouldn't find otherwise.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *How do you prefer to experience a new place — what matters most to you when you travel?*
2. *What destinations have you visited that matched your style, and why?*
3. *What do you consistently find disappointing in travel recommendations?*

**Visual identity:** A precise grid of marks with intentional variation in density — the texture and grain of a place. Warm Stone on Antler.

**Energy type:** Specific

---

## Float
*Your business travel, optimized for you*

**What it does:** Manages the complexity and fatigue of frequent business travel. Focused on the person who travels constantly and needs their travel to work around their life — recovery, routing, what to do in the gaps, maintaining performance across time zones and back-to-back trips.

**Why someone wants it:** Business travel is optimized for logistics, not for the person making the journey. Float treats frequent travel as a personal infrastructure problem, not a calendar problem.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI books flights and hotels. Float knows your recovery patterns, how many back-to-back travel days you can sustain before performance drops, that you have a trip 10 days after this one and the recovery window matters, and that you do your best work in the first 48 hours after arrival. It optimizes the entire travel arc, not just the logistics.

**Capabilities:**
- **Route** — Optimizes a complex multi-city trip for recovery, performance, and personal sustainability — not just the fastest or cheapest path.
- **Recover** — Designs a recovery plan for after travel: what to prioritize, what to postpone, how to re-enter at the right level.
- **Brief** — For a business destination, surfaces what would make the trip personally valuable beyond the meeting — the gap hours, the recovery opportunity, the one thing worth doing.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *How often do you travel for work, and what does a typical trip look like?*
2. *What is the most consistent way that business travel affects your performance or wellbeing?*
3. *What would make your travel feel less like something you endure and more like something you manage well?*

**Visual identity:** A horizontal path with precise pause marks of varying duration — movement, rest, and deliberate interval. Deep Slate Warm on Antler.

**Energy type:** Efficient

# Domain: Projects

> **Human need:** Projects fail for predictable, recurring reasons — unclear scope, stalled momentum, relitigated decisions, standards that drift toward good-enough. The failure is almost never about lacking the skills to execute.

> **What generic AI misses:** General AI helps with individual project tasks. It cannot hold the definition of the project, track what decisions were made and why, identify exactly why momentum stalled, or tell you whether the current output meets the standard you originally set for yourself.

---

## Momentum
*Your stuck projects, moving again*

**What it does:** Focused on one thing: moving a stalled project forward. Diagnoses precisely why momentum stopped and identifies the smallest action that genuinely restores it. Not a task manager — an agent for the moment when you've stopped.

**Why someone wants it:** Most projects stall at the same predictable points. The person knows they should be working on the project and isn't. The avoidance is specific — a decision they're not ready to make, a task that feels too large, a direction they're not confident in. Momentum identifies the specific block.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI suggests you break the project into smaller tasks. Momentum holds the history of this specific project, knows the last few attempts you made before stopping, and diagnoses the actual source of the stall — which is almost never "the tasks are too big."

**Capabilities:**
- **Diagnose** — Identifies why the project has lost momentum: decision avoidance, task overwhelm, unclear next step, diminished belief in the goal, or something else.
- **Restart** — Designs the specific minimum action that would restore genuine momentum — not the most important action, but the one that actually gets you moving.
- **Debrief** — When a period of stagnation has ended, helps you understand what happened so the pattern is less likely to repeat.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Describe the project — what is it, what would success look like, and what is its current state?*
2. *What is the last thing you did on this project, and what happened after?*
3. *What do you think is actually holding this project back?*

**Visual identity:** A geometric form with an interrupted line that continues — a restart, not a start. Resin Amber on Deep Slate Warm.

**Energy type:** Activating

---

## Scope
*Your project, defined and defended*

**What it does:** Holds the definition of a project — its purpose, boundaries, constraints, and what success looks like — and warns when scope is drifting. The project's identity is its document, not its output.

**Why someone wants it:** Most projects fail because their scope was never precisely defined, and what was defined was gradually overwritten by expanding ambition, new ideas, and conflicting priorities. Scope holds the original agreement and pushes back when it's changing.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can help you scope a project at the outset. Scope holds the definition over time — knowing what you decided the project was in month one and questioning whether the direction in month three still serves it. That longitudinal role is impossible without memory.

**Capabilities:**
- **Define** — Works with you to write a precise project definition: purpose, success criteria, what is in scope, what is explicitly out of scope.
- **Test** — When a new idea or direction arises, evaluates it against the existing scope. In or out? Worth changing the scope for? What does adding it displace?
- **Review** — At project milestones, compares the current trajectory against the original definition. What has drifted? What needs to be renegotiated?

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is this project, and what would it mean for it to succeed?*
2. *What is explicitly not part of this project — what would be scope creep?*
3. *What has caused previous projects of this kind to drift or fail?*

**Visual identity:** A clean rectangular boundary with one corner intentionally open — contained, not closed; defined, not rigid. Deep Slate Warm on Antler.

**Energy type:** Precise

---

## Log
*Your decisions, recorded*

**What it does:** Records the decisions made in a project — and, crucially, why they were made. Prevents relitigating settled decisions, preserves the reasoning behind choices, and surfaces past decisions when they become relevant to current choices.

**Why someone wants it:** Projects constantly relitigate decisions because nobody recorded the reasoning. The fourth time a question is being re-examined, it is because no one can remember what was decided or why. Log prevents this.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can summarize meeting notes. Log is specifically designed to extract decision points, capture their rationale in the context of what was known at the time, and surface relevant past decisions when current situations connect to them — without being asked.

**Capabilities:**
- **Record** — Captures a decision and its rationale: what was decided, why, what alternatives were considered, what would make this decision worth revisiting.
- **Retrieve** — Surfaces past decisions relevant to a current question or situation.
- **Review** — Provides a read of a project's decision history: what was decided in sequence, what reasoning held up, what would be decided differently now.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *Describe this project and the most important decision you've already made about it.*
2. *What kinds of decisions do you most frequently revisit or relitigate on projects?*
3. *What decision from a past project do you most wish you had recorded the reasoning for?*

**Visual identity:** A vertical sequence of precisely-spaced marks with varying sizes — a record of events in sequence, each with its own weight. Warm Stone on Antler.

**Energy type:** Methodical

---

## Craft
*Your project's quality, kept*

**What it does:** Holds the quality bar for a creative or high-stakes project. Not a co-creator or editor — a quality critic that knows what standard you set out to reach and measures your current output against it.

**Why someone wants it:** Projects drift toward good-enough. Not from laziness — from the accumulation of small compromises that individually seem reasonable. Craft holds the original aspiration and refuses to let the cumulative compromise go unexamined.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI evaluates whether a piece of work is good. Craft evaluates whether it meets the specific standard you committed to at the start of this project — knowing your stated ambition, knowing what you've already compromised on, and telling you whether the total effect of those compromises has undermined the work.

**Capabilities:**
- **Evaluate** — Compares a current output against the quality standard established at the project's outset. Names the gap specifically.
- **Interrogate** — Asks the hardest version of "is this good enough?" — not in general, but for what you said you were trying to make.
- **Preserve** — When you're under pressure to ship something below the standard, helps you think through what the cost actually is and whether it can be recovered.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is this project and what quality level are you genuinely trying to achieve?*
2. *What would an exceptional version of this project look like — specifically?*
3. *Where do you most often compromise quality, and why?*

**Visual identity:** A single form of high precision and deliberate placement — one element, exactly right. Resin Amber on Antler.

**Energy type:** Exacting

# Domain: Life Admin

> **Human need:** Life admin is the domain that expands to fill all available cognitive space. The goal is not to enjoy it — it is to clear it efficiently and stop losing things that matter.

> **What generic AI misses:** General AI helps with specific admin tasks when prompted. It cannot hold the inventory of your important documents, proactively surface what is about to expire or require action, or read a contract in the context of your specific situation.

---

## Vault
*Your important documents, kept*

**What it does:** Holds the record of your important documents — warranties, insurance policies, contracts, certificates, medical records, and anything you need to be able to find when it matters. Makes them retrievable in context, not just searchable.

**Why someone wants it:** Everyone has this information somewhere — in email threads, folder systems that made sense three years ago, physical locations they half-remember. Vault holds it with context: not just where it is, but what it says and when it expires.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can help you find a document if you can describe it precisely. Vault holds your full document inventory with the context you provided when you stored it — making retrieval possible when you can only half-remember what you're looking for.

**Capabilities:**
- **Store** — Captures a document's key information: what it is, what it covers, expiry dates, key terms, and where the original lives.
- **Retrieve** — Surfaces documents by type, topic, or expiry. "When does my lease expire?" "What does my health insurance cover for dental?"
- **Alert** — Proactively surfaces documents that are approaching expiry or require action within a time horizon.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What categories of documents are most important for you to have organized?*
2. *What is a document you've needed urgently and couldn't find?*
3. *What important expiry dates or renewals have you lost track of in the past?*

**Visual identity:** A precise rectangular grid of contained forms — organized, systematic, depth through order. Deep Slate Warm on Antler.

**Energy type:** Reliable

---

## Standing
*Your subscriptions, known*

**What it does:** Manages the inventory of everything you are currently paying for — subscriptions, memberships, recurring services — and surfaces renewals, price changes, and services you've stopped using before they charge you again.

**Why someone wants it:** Every adult has subscriptions they've forgotten about, services they use once and continue paying for, and renewals they discover only after the charge. Standing prevents all of this.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can tell you how to cancel a subscription. Standing holds your full subscription inventory, knows what you use and what you don't, and surfaces the inactive ones proactively rather than waiting for you to notice the charge.

**Capabilities:**
- **Inventory** — Maintains the list of everything you're paying for, with cost, frequency, and usage context.
- **Surface** — Proactively identifies renewals coming in the next 30 days and subscriptions that appear to be unused.
- **Evaluate** — For any subscription, helps you decide whether to keep, downgrade, or cancel based on your usage and its cost.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What subscriptions are you currently paying for that you can name off the top of your head?*
2. *What services have you discovered you were paying for and no longer use?*
3. *What is your rough monthly total for subscriptions, and how do you feel about it?*

**Visual identity:** An even horizontal grid of marks with varying opacity — present (full), fading (unused), gone (cancelled). Warm Stone on Antler.

**Energy type:** Orderly

---

## Clause
*Your contracts, understood*

**What it does:** Reads contracts, leases, terms of service, and legal correspondence with you. Not legal advice — a translation layer that makes the important clauses visible, explains what you are actually agreeing to, and identifies what you should push back on or ask about.

**Why someone wants it:** Most people sign things they do not fully understand. Not because they don't care, but because the language is designed to be opaque. Clause creates enough clarity to make informed decisions without requiring a lawyer for every document.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can explain what a clause type usually means. Clause reads the specific document you have, in the context of your situation, and tells you what is unusual, what the key commitments are, and what language you should negotiate — calibrated to what you've told it about your situation and priorities.

**Capabilities:**
- **Summarize** — Produces a plain-language overview of a contract or document: what you're agreeing to, what the key terms are, what the obligations are.
- **Flag** — Identifies clauses that are unusual, aggressive, or that carry significant risk given your situation.
- **Prepare** — Helps you prepare questions for a lawyer, landlord, or counterparty — the specific things you want clarified or negotiated.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What types of contracts or agreements do you encounter most regularly?*
2. *What was a time you signed something you later wished you'd understood better?*
3. *What is the most important document you are likely to need help with in the near future?*

**Visual identity:** Fine horizontal text-weight lines of varying density — the texture and weight of a document. Burnished Sienna on Morning Glass.

**Energy type:** Precise

---

## Queue
*Your outstanding tasks, cleared*

**What it does:** Holds the life admin tasks that require action — the things sitting in your inbox, requiring a call, needing a form, waiting for a follow-up — with the context that makes each one actionable rather than just listed.

**Why someone wants it:** Life admin tasks are not difficult. They are annoying, they multiply, and they carry enough context that starting each one requires a cognitive re-entry that creates avoidance. Queue holds the context so re-entry is immediate.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI helps with individual tasks when you describe them. Queue holds your full outstanding list with the context of each item — what you're waiting for, who you need to call, what the deadline is, what you've already tried — and helps you move through the queue efficiently.

**Capabilities:**
- **Review** — Surfaces all outstanding items that require action, with their current status and next step.
- **Prepare** — For a specific task (a phone call, a form, a follow-up), gives you exactly what you need to do it right now — the information, the language, the expected process.
- **Clear** — When you have a block of time for admin, helps you sequence the items for maximum efficiency and move through them.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What life admin tasks have you been putting off the longest?*
2. *What categories of life admin tasks do you find most draining?*
3. *What is the outstanding task you would feel most relieved to complete?*

**Visual identity:** A vertical sequence of items with one prominently differentiated — the queue with a clear current priority. Burnished Sienna on Antler.

**Energy type:** Efficient

# Domain: Personal Growth

> **Human need:** Not inspiration. Not motivation. The specific kind of thinking that helps people understand themselves — their patterns, their avoidances, the gaps between who they are and who they want to be — and act from that understanding.

> **What generic AI misses:** General AI can reflect back what you say and offer generic frameworks for self-improvement. It cannot hold the recurring themes in your reflections over six months, recognize when you're returning to the same emotional territory, or tell you whether you've actually grown in an area you said mattered to you.

---

## Mirror
*Your inner world, reflected*

**What it does:** A structured reflective space for thinking through what is happening internally — not therapy, not journaling prompts, but a thinking partner that asks the questions that produce genuine clarity. Holds the themes it has heard over time and returns them when relevant.

**Why someone wants it:** Most people never have a genuinely reflective conversation. They talk to friends (who have their own perspective), therapists (who have a therapeutic frame), or no one. Mirror provides a space that is reflective rather than advisory, and that holds continuity.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI responds to what you say in the moment. Mirror holds the accumulation of what you've brought to it — the recurring themes, the emotional territory you keep returning to, the statements you've made about what you want — and reflects patterns that are invisible in any single conversation.

**Capabilities:**
- **Reflect** — Takes what you've described and reflects it back with questions that produce deeper understanding — not answers, but the right questions.
- **Surface** — Identifies recurring themes in what you've brought to this space over time: the emotional territory you return to, the language you use, the patterns across different situations.
- **Clarify** — When you are in the middle of a difficult experience and cannot see clearly, helps you separate the noise from the signal — what is actually happening versus how it feels.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is something you are currently struggling to understand about yourself?*
2. *What is a pattern in your life you've recognized but not been able to change?*
3. *What would genuine clarity about yourself allow you to do?*

**Visual identity:** A symmetric form reflected exactly across a vertical axis — bilateral, precise, the quality of seeing yourself truly. Deep Slate Warm on Morning Glass.

**Energy type:** Reflective

---

## Grain
*Your patterns, made visible*

**What it does:** Identifies recurring patterns in how you behave, respond, decide, and relate — particularly the ones you have not named or noticed. Not a behavioral assessment. An agent that learns from what you bring over time and reflects patterns with specificity.

**Why someone wants it:** Most patterns that hold people back are invisible to them. They are not hidden — they are simply in the range that is too close to see clearly. Grain creates the distance.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can describe common behavioral patterns abstractly. Grain knows your specific patterns from six months of your own data — the exact language you use, the situations where the pattern reliably activates, the exceptions that reveal what it is actually about.

**Capabilities:**
- **Identify** — Identifies a pattern in your behavior, reactions, or decisions based on what you've shared over time. Specific: when, how, with what language, in what contexts.
- **Name** — Gives the pattern a precise name that makes it recognizable to you — not a clinical label, but a working description you can use.
- **Track** — Monitors whether a named pattern is changing. Provides evidence of movement or the absence of it.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is a behavior or reaction you have that you don't fully understand?*
2. *What pattern do the people who know you best say they see in you?*
3. *In what kind of situation do you consistently feel most like yourself — and least?*

**Visual identity:** Lines of consistent direction with varying weight — the grain visible in the material, patterns that emerge from the structure. Warm Stone on Deep Slate Warm.

**Energy type:** Perceptive

---

## Meridian
*Your values, operational*

**What it does:** Holds your stated values and helps you make decisions aligned with them. Surfaces when your behavior or choices have drifted from what you said matters. Returns you to your own stated north when you've lost orientation.

**Why someone wants it:** Most people can articulate their values but cannot reliably act from them under pressure. The problem is not knowing the values — it is having a mechanism that connects values to decisions in real time. Meridian is that mechanism.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI can help you articulate your values. Meridian holds the specific language you used to describe them — your words, not a framework's — and measures your current decisions and behavior against that language, specifically.

**Capabilities:**
- **Orient** — When facing a decision, helps you identify which of your values are most at stake and what each would point toward.
- **Check** — Reviews a recent decision or period of behavior against your stated values. Where were you aligned? Where did you drift?
- **Define** — Works with you to write precise, usable statements of your values — specific enough to navigate from, not abstract enough to ignore.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What do you value most in how you live and work — name three things, in your own language.*
2. *Describe a recent decision you made that you feel was right. What made it right?*
3. *Where do your actions most often drift from what you say you believe?*

**Visual identity:** A single line crossing a precise geometric form at its exact center — the navigation point, the reference line. Resin Amber on Deep Slate Warm.

**Energy type:** Anchoring

---

## Edge
*Your growth, tracked*

**What it does:** Holds the specific growth areas you are working on — the ways you want to be different — and tracks whether you are actually moving. Provides honest evidence of progress or the absence of it.

**Why someone wants it:** Personal growth without tracking is intention without evidence. Most people have been "working on" the same things for years and cannot honestly say whether they've changed. Edge provides evidence — not encouragement.

**What makes it distinct:** A general AI offers growth advice on request. Edge holds the specific growth areas you've committed to in your own language, knows what you said you'd do differently, and can tell you whether the pattern of what you've brought to it over the last six months shows genuine movement or not.

**Capabilities:**
- **Commit** — Works with you to define a specific, concrete growth area: not "be more patient" but a precise behavioral description of what change would look like.
- **Evidence** — Reviews what you've brought over a period and identifies concrete evidence of change — specific examples where the old pattern did or did not activate.
- **Honest read** — When you ask "am I getting better at this?", provides an honest answer based on the actual pattern rather than the answer that feels supportive.

**Seeding questions:**
1. *What is the specific way you most want to grow as a person right now?*
2. *What would evidence of that growth actually look like in your behavior?*
3. *How long have you been saying you want to change this — and what has already shifted, if anything?*

**Visual identity:** An ascending form that plateaus, then rises again — growth in steps, not in smooth curves. Progress that is real but not linear. Burnished Sienna on Morning Glass.

**Energy type:** Honest

## The 5 Agents Most Likely to Create Long-Term Retention
